The Real Estate Academy
Development Standard 07
Project Standard
Version 1.0

Purpose
This document establishes the minimum quality standard for every Academy Project. Projects transform knowledge into professional capability by requiring learners to complete authentic work that mirrors the responsibilities of practicing real estate professionals.

Design Philosophy
Projects are demonstrations of competence rather than academic exercises. Every project should solve a realistic problem, encourage independent thinking, integrate multiple competencies, and produce work a learner would be proud to include in a professional portfolio.

Required Sections
1. Project Mission
Describe the professional capability this project is intended to develop.

2. Real-World Context
Explain how this project reflects authentic real estate practice.

3. Learning Outcomes
Define measurable skills and competencies demonstrated upon completion.

4. Project Brief
Provide a detailed description of the task, client scenario, or business challenge.

5. Required Deliverables
Clearly identify every document, presentation, analysis, workflow, or artifact learners must produce.

6. Constraints & Assumptions
Define realistic boundaries such as budget, timeline, regulations, market conditions, or client requirements.

7. AI Integration
Describe how AI may responsibly assist while preserving original thinking, ethics, and professional judgment.

8. Ontario Context
Identify Ontario legislation, standards, and professional expectations relevant to the project.

9. Success Criteria
Describe what excellent work looks like before learners begin.

10. Evaluation Rubric
Assess using the Academy's four dimensions: Knowledge, Professional Judgment, Communication, and Ethics & Client Focus.

11. Reflection
Require learners to evaluate what they learned, what challenged them, and what they would improve.

12. Portfolio Value
Explain how the completed project contributes to the learner's professional portfolio.

13. Mentor Notes
Share practical advice, common pitfalls, and real-world insights.

14. Future Expansion
Record ideas for more advanced project variants or industry updates.

15. Revision History
Track meaningful changes using semantic versioning.

Project Quality Standard
Feels authentic and professionally relevant.
Integrates multiple Academy competencies.
Requires critical thinking and independent decision-making.
Produces tangible deliverables.
Encourages ethical reasoning.
Supports portfolio development.
Provides value beyond licensing.
Aligns with the Founding Papers and Curriculum Master Plan.

The Four-Dimensional Evaluation
Knowledge – Demonstrates conceptual understanding.
Professional Judgment – Makes sound, client-centered decisions.
Communication – Communicates clearly, professionally, and persuasively.
Ethics & Client Focus – Acts with integrity and prioritizes the client's best interests.

Guiding Principle
Every Academy Project should produce work that reflects the quality, professionalism, and judgment expected from a trusted Realtor. Upon completion, learners should have created something worthy of discussing in an interview, sharing with a mentor, or adding to a professional portfolio.
