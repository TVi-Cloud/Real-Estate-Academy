Development Standard 06
Assessment Standard
Version 1.0

Purpose
This document establishes the minimum quality standard for every assessment within The Real Estate Academy. Assessments measure understanding, professional judgment, ethical reasoning, communication, and practical application—not memorization alone.

Design Philosophy
Assessment is part of learning, not merely a measurement at the end. Every assessment should reinforce concepts, encourage reflection, and provide meaningful feedback that helps learners improve.

Required Sections
1. Assessment Purpose
State why this assessment exists and which competencies it measures.

2. Learning Outcomes Assessed
Identify the specific outcomes being evaluated.

3. Assessment Format
Describe the assessment structure (quiz, short answer, scenario, presentation, reflection, practical exercise, etc.).

4. Instructions
Provide clear expectations, submission requirements, and success criteria.

5. Assessment Content
Present questions or activities using a progression from foundational understanding to professional application.

6. Scenario-Based Evaluation
Include realistic situations requiring professional judgment whenever appropriate.

7. Reflection Component
Encourage learners to evaluate their own reasoning, confidence, and areas for improvement.

8. Evaluation Rubric
Define how work will be assessed using transparent criteria.

9. Feedback Strategy
Specify how constructive feedback should guide further learning.

10. Ontario Context
Include Ontario-specific considerations where appropriate.

11. AI Considerations
State whether AI may be used, how it may be used responsibly, and which work must remain the learner's own.

12. Common Misconceptions
Identify frequent errors and learning opportunities.

13. Revision History
Track updates using semantic versioning.

Assessment Quality Standard
Measures understanding before recall.
Evaluates professional judgment and ethics.
Encourages critical thinking.
Includes practical application.
Provides actionable feedback.
Supports long-term competence.
Aligns with the Founding Papers and Curriculum Master Plan.
Maintains fairness, clarity, and transparency.

Assessment Principles
Assess for growth, not perfection.
Reward reasoning, not guesswork.
Value communication as highly as technical accuracy.
Use mistakes as opportunities for learning.
Assess the ability to serve clients—not simply pass exams.

Include a rating for each of the 4 dimensions listed below.

Example:
* 🧠 Knowledge: 9.5 / 10
* ⚖️ Professional Judgment: 8.8 / 10
* 💬 Communication: 9.2 / 10
* ❤️ Ethics & Client Focus: 10 / 10

Guiding Principle
An exceptional assessment should leave learners more capable than when they began. The goal is not to prove what they know, but to strengthen how they think, decide, and serve.
