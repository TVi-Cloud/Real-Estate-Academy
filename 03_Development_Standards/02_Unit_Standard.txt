Unit Standard
Version 1.0

Purpose
This document establishes the minimum quality standard for every Unit within The Real Estate Academy. A Unit is a coherent collection of Learning Experiences that develops one major competency within a Module. Every Unit must contribute directly to the Module Mission and prepare learners for the next stage of their educational journey.

Design Philosophy
A Unit is not simply a collection of lessons. It is a progression. Every Learning Experience should naturally prepare the learner for the next one. By the end of a Unit, students should have mastered one meaningful area of professional competence before moving forward.

Required Sections
1. Unit Mission
Describe the transformation this Unit is intended to produce.

2. Big Question
One guiding question that anchors every Learning Experience in the Unit.

3. Why This Unit Matters
Explain the practical value of mastering this competency and how it improves client outcomes.

4. Learning Outcomes
Describe what learners will be able to do by the end of the Unit using action-oriented outcomes.

5. Core Competencies
Identify knowledge, communication, ethics, AI, client service, critical thinking, and professional judgment developed.

6. Learning Experience Roadmap
List every Learning Experience in sequence with estimated time, difficulty, and prerequisites.

7. Vocabulary
List and organize essential terminology introduced within the Unit.

8. Ontario Context
Explain how this Unit relates specifically to Ontario legislation, standards, and practice where appropriate.

9. Professional Insight
Capture how experienced professionals think differently than beginners regarding this competency.

10. AI Integration Strategy
Describe responsible ways AI enhances learning and professional practice without replacing judgment.

11. Unit Case Studies
List the practical case studies included in the Unit.

12. Academy Project
Define one substantial real-world project demonstrating mastery.

13. Assessment Strategy
Specify quizzes, reflections, scenarios, practical evaluations, and project assessments.

14. Completion Criteria
Define the standard required to successfully complete the Unit.

15. Connections
Reference previous Units, future Units, related Modules, Academy Projects, and the Capstone.

16. Instructor Notes
Internal curriculum guidance including sequencing rationale, misconceptions, teaching notes, and future improvements.

17. Future Expansion
Record ideas for additional Learning Experiences, industry trends, technology, legislation, and research.

18. Revision History
Track meaningful changes using semantic versioning.

Unit Quality Standard
Supports the Founding Papers.
Aligns with the Curriculum Master Plan.
Contributes directly to the Module Mission.
Builds long-term professional competence.
Integrates ethics and human connection.
Uses Ontario-specific context where appropriate.
Incorporates AI responsibly.
Emphasizes practical application.
Provides lasting value beyond licensing.

Guiding Principle
A Unit should not simply organize information. It should develop one professional capability that students will carry with them throughout their careers.
