Development Standard 03
Learning Experience Standard
Version 1.0

Purpose
This document establishes the minimum quality standard for every Learning Experience within The Real Estate Academy. A Learning Experience is the Academy's primary educational building block. Its purpose is not merely to transfer information, but to create understanding, develop professional judgment, build confidence, encourage ethical decision-making, and prepare learners for real-world application.

Design Philosophy
A Learning Experience is not a lecture. It is an experience. Every Learning Experience should engage the learner intellectually, professionally, and personally. Knowledge should lead to understanding. Understanding should lead to confidence. Confidence should lead to action.

Required Sections
1. Mission
Describe the transformation this Learning Experience is intended to produce.

2. Big Question
One guiding question that anchors the entire Learning Experience.

3. Why This Matters
Explain why the learner should care and how this improves client outcomes.

4. Learning Objectives
Use action-oriented outcomes such as explain, demonstrate, apply, evaluate, recommend, and create.

5. Key Vocabulary
Introduce professional terminology using clear, plain-language definitions.

6. Foundations
Teach concepts from first principles, building intuition before complexity.

7. Ontario Context
Relate the topic to Ontario legislation, standards, and professional practice where appropriate.

8. Professional Insight
Explain how experienced professionals think differently than beginners.

9. Practical Example
Bridge theory to practice using realistic situations.

10. Case Study
Provide a realistic scenario for learners to analyze before reviewing the recommended approach.

11. AI Lab
Show responsible ways AI can improve research, organization, communication, marketing, and productivity while preserving professional judgment.

12. Reflection
Conclude with three reflections: Knowledge, Professional Judgment, and Action.

13. Knowledge Check
Use a mixture of multiple-choice, short-answer, and scenario-based questions.

14. Academy Assignment
Provide one practical, real-world exercise demonstrating application.

15. Connections
Reference previous and future Learning Experiences, related Units, Modules, books, and resources.

16. Mentor Notes
Capture practical wisdom, common mistakes, habits, and insights that rarely appear in textbooks.

17. Additional Resources
List books, articles, videos, Ontario references, AI prompts, and further reading.

18. Instructor Notes
Internal guidance including sequencing rationale, misconceptions, and future improvements.

19. Future Expansion
Record ideas for additional content, legislation updates, guest contributors, and emerging technologies.

20. Revision History
Track changes using semantic versioning.

Learning Experience Quality Standard
Aligns with the Founding Papers.
Supports the Curriculum Master Plan.
Reinforces the Unit Mission.
Develops long-term professional competence.
Encourages ethical decision-making.
Builds confidence through practical application.
Integrates AI responsibly.
Uses Ontario-specific context where appropriate.
Prioritizes understanding over memorization.
Provides value well beyond licensing.

The Three Outcomes
Every Learning Experience must produce three outcomes:

Knowledge — The learner understands something new.

Professional Judgment — The learner thinks differently.

Action — The learner is prepared to act differently.

If one of these outcomes is missing, the Learning Experience is incomplete.

Guiding Principle
A great Learning Experience does more than teach information. It changes how a professional sees the world, serves clients, and approaches their craft.
