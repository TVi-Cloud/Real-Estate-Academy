Case Study Standard
Version 1.0

Purpose
This document establishes the minimum quality standard for every Case Study within The Real Estate Academy. Case Studies bridge theory and practice by placing learners into realistic professional situations that require analysis, judgment, communication, ethics, and decision-making.

Design Philosophy
A Case Study is not an example with an obvious answer. It is an authentic scenario where learners must think like trusted professionals before reviewing expert guidance.

Required Sections
1. Learning Purpose
State why this case study exists and which competencies it develops.

2. Scenario Overview
Introduce the client, situation, and context without revealing the solution.

3. Client Profile
Describe goals, concerns, finances, timeline, and relevant background.

4. Supporting Information
Provide listings, market conditions, documents, emails, timelines, or other realistic information.

5. Decision Points
Identify key moments where learners must make professional decisions.

6. Reflection Questions
Prompt learners to justify their reasoning before viewing guidance.

7. Professional Analysis
Present an expert breakdown explaining possible approaches, trade-offs, and risks.

8. Ontario Considerations
Highlight legislation, ethics, disclosure, or regulatory issues relevant to the scenario.

9. AI Integration
Show how AI could responsibly assist with research, organization, communication, or preparation while preserving professional judgment.

10. Common Mistakes
Identify errors frequently made by new professionals and how to avoid them.

11. Mentor Notes
Share practical wisdom and experience that extends beyond textbooks.

12. Extension Challenge
Offer a variation that changes assumptions and encourages deeper thinking.

13. Revision History
Track meaningful updates using semantic versioning.

Case Study Quality Standard
Feels authentic and professionally realistic.
Requires critical thinking rather than memorization.
Encourages ethical decision-making.
Reflects Ontario practice where appropriate.
Allows multiple reasonable approaches when appropriate.
Builds confidence through practical application.
Connects directly to the Learning Experience and Unit.

Guiding Principle
A great Case Study allows learners to experience professional judgment before they encounter it in real life. It transforms knowledge into wisdom through thoughtful practice.
